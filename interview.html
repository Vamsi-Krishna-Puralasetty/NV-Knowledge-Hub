Group Discussion

What is a Group Discussion (GD)?

A Group Discussion (GD) is a method used by organizations, colleges, and companies to assess candidates' communication skills, teamwork, problem-solving ability, and knowledge on a specific topic. In a GD, a group of participants discusses a given topic within a set time.

The evaluators observe and assess each participant's performance based on their ability to present ideas clearly, listen to others, and contribute meaningfully.

Dos for a Group Discussion

  • Prepare in Advance: Research common GD topics (current affairs, abstract topics, or case studies). Be aware of general knowledge and trending issues.
  • Listen Actively: Pay attention to what others are saying to build on their points or present counterarguments respectfully.
  • Communicate Clearly: Speak confidently, maintain a good pace, and articulate your points in a structured manner.
  • Take Initiative: If appropriate, start the discussion with a strong and relevant point to stand out.
  • Be a Team Player: Encourage quieter participants to speak and maintain a collaborative approach throughout the discussion.

Dont's for a Group Discussion

  • Interrupt Others: Avoid cutting people off while theyâ€™re speaking. Wait for your turn.
  • Dominate the Discussion: Don't monopolize the conversation; let others express their views too.
  • Be Aggressive: Disagree politely instead of arguing or being disrespectful.
  • Use Jargon or Complex Words: Avoid language that may confuse others. Keep it simple and precise.
  • Go Off-Topic: Stay focused on the subject matter and avoid unnecessary digressions.

How to Prepare for a Group Discussion

  1. Stay updated on current affairs, social issues, and trending topics by reading newspapers, blogs, and magazines.
  2. Join study groups or participate in GDs with friends to simulate real scenarios.
  3. Work on your vocabulary, pronunciation, and clarity of speech.
  4. Practice framing your points using formats like SPELT (Social, Political, Economic, Legal, Technological) or PREP (Point, Reason, Example, Point).
  5. Analyze different perspectives of a topic and be prepared to discuss them.
